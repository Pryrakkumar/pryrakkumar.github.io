Research Interests

I am interested in how meteorological variables and human perception interact with urban air quality. My current work explores PM2.5 exposure, public risk perception, and the role of temperature, rainfall, and solar radiation in shaping pollution levels.

  • Air pollution and PM2.5 exposure
  • Environmental and public health impacts of poor air quality
  • Urban air quality monitoring and perception studies
  • R-based statistical analysis and visualization

R Analysis Projects

1. Correlation Analysis of PM2.5 and Meteorology

Computed Pearson correlations between PM2.5 and temperature, rainfall, and solar radiation to identify the strongest drivers of air quality in a sample dataset.

2. Simple & Multiple Regression Models

Built linear regression models to predict PM2.5 using single predictors and a combined model with temperature, rainfall, and solar radiation. Evaluated model fit using RÂ², adjusted RÂ², and significance tests (p-values, F-statistic).

3. Time-Series Visualization

Created time-series plots to visualize daily variations in PM2.5 and meteorological variables, using ggplot2 with custom themes, labels, and date formatting.

4. Regression Diagnostics

Performed diagnostic checks including Q-Q plots, residual vs fitted plots, Scale-Location plots, Shapiro-Wilk test, and Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) to validate model assumptions.

5. Research Pipeline (Work in Progress)

Developing a reusable R script to automate: data import, cleaning, merging meteorological and PM2.5 datasets, generating plots, computing correlations, and running regression models for longer time periods.

Publication

Public Perception of Air Pollution Exposure and Health Impacts in Central Delhi

Kumar, P., & Abhishek, A. (2026). Journal of Emerging Technologies and Innovative Research, 13(2).
DOI: 10.56975/jetir.v13i2.575762

This study investigates how residents in Central Delhi perceive air pollution and its health impacts, and compares these perceptions with measured PM2.5 levels. Findings highlight gaps between perceived and actual risk, underlining the importance of effective communication and policy interventions.
