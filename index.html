Research Interests

I am interested in how meteorological variables and public perception interact with urban air quality. My current work explores PM2.5 exposure, risk perception, and the influence of temperature, rainfall, and solar radiation on pollution levels using R.

  • Air pollution and PM2.5 exposure in urban environments
  • Environmental and public health impacts of poor air quality
  • Urban air quality monitoring and perception-based studies
  • R-based statistical analysis and data visualization

Publication

Public Perception of Air Pollution Exposure and Health Impacts in Central Delhi

Kumar, P., & Abhishek, A. (2026), Journal of Emerging Technologies and Innovative Research, 13(2).
DOI: 10.56975/jetir.v13i2.575762

This study investigates how residents in Central Delhi perceive air pollution and its health impacts, and compares these perceptions with measured PM2.5 levels. The findings highlight gaps between perceived and actual risk, emphasizing the importance of effective communication and evidence-based policy.
